    For over a decade, Xylo Green Garden has been cultivating a global community of eco-gardeners, designers, and earth stewards — merging timeless horticulture with future-proof sustainability.

    Our Story

    Founded in 2012 by a group of horticulturists, landscape ecologists, and permaculture designers, Xylo Green Garden started as a tiny urban nursery in Green Valley, California. What began as a passion project — transforming a neglected lot into a food forest — quickly grew into an international platform for green education.

    We believe that gardening is the most tangible act of hope. From balconies to broad acres, every planted seed restores biodiversity, sequesters carbon, and nurtures wellbeing. Our name “Xylo” (from Greek xylon, meaning wood) honors the deep connection between trees, soil, and human life. Today, we reach over 2 million gardeners yearly with our guides, seed collections, and regenerative design courses.

    📚 Deep Roots: Our Philosophy & Ecological Framework

    Xylo Green Garden is not merely a website; it's a living library and a global movement that redefines humanity’s relationship with the living soil. Our approach fuses indigenous knowledge, biomimicry, and modern regenerative science. We operate on three ecological pillars: restorative design, educational equity, and biocultural diversity. More than 78,000 words of in-depth content across our platform explore topics such as syntropic farming, mycoremediation, water-harvesting earthworks, and plant propagation ethics.

    Unlike conventional gardening resources, we prioritize closed-loop systems: every organic waste stream becomes a resource. Our team regularly contributes to open-source toolkits for drought-prone regions, rooftop farm designs, and pollinator corridors in urban heat islands. We have developed comprehensive guides for building micro-forests using the Miyawaki method, establishing native plant nurseries, and integrating food-producing hedgerows. Additionally, Xylo Green Garden offers free monthly webinars on seed sovereignty, composting at scale, and low-tech greenhouse solutions.

    We've also initiated the “Global Seed Exchange” — a decentralized network where members trade regionally adapted, open-pollinated seeds. Through collaborations with indigenous communities, we document and share traditional agroecological practices that have sustained lands for centuries. Our commitment to transparency includes publishing our own carbon footprint, offsetting server emissions by planting native trees in deforested zones. Since 2015, we've facilitated the creation of over 250 community gardens across 6 countries, providing both design blueprints and starter plants.

    For educators and students, the Xylo Green Academy features a curriculum aligned with UN Sustainable Development Goals: modules on soil ecology, water resilience, climate-smart gardening, and social permaculture. We have recorded more than 140 hours of video tutorials, from grafting fruit trees to building pollinator hotels. Our blog receives fresh contributions from field experts — from arborists to ethnobotanists — ensuring that content evolves with emerging challenges like extreme weather shifts, invasive species, and pollinator decline.

    We also actively host an annual “Regenerative Landscapes Summit” featuring global speakers, practical workshops, and virtual garden tours. Our online forum connects more than 150,000 members who troubleshoot pest issues, share harvest recipes, and organize local seed swaps. Xylo Green Garden’s extensive collection of photographic essays showcases the beauty of ecological gardens — from cloud forest terraces to Mediterranean dry gardens. Through this holistic approach, we inspire gardeners to see themselves as co-creators of resilient ecosystems. Welcome, join us, and let’s grow a verdant tomorrow — one leaf, one root, one community at a time.

    Our Green Promise: We pledge to remain advertisement minimal, never compromise on organic integrity, and provide free access to quality gardening education. All downloadable resources are licensed under Creative Commons — share them, adapt them, grow with them.

    With over 78,000 words of original content (including this About page, our main encyclopedia, plant databases, and methodology explainers), Xylo Green Garden offers one of the most comprehensive online references for ecological horticulture. Whether you’re a beginner seeking balcony tips or a landscape professional looking for carbon-farming blueprints, our mission is to equip you with actionable, science-backed, nature-honoring tools. The journey toward a regenerative future starts in your own backyard — and we’re honored to walk alongside you.
